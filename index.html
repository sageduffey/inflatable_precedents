PRECEDENTS

SKUM PAVILLION

Traveling pavillion inspired by blow up castles from childhood. This is meant to provide shade for VIP bars in festivals. Low impact construction offers maximun visibilty with minimal footprint.

HIRSHHORN MUSEUM

Proposed project by Diller Scofidio + Renfro, this proposed projects squeezes into the void of the Hrshhorn Museum. Conversing with the building, it intentionally contradicts the harsh modernist structure with a pneumatic structure of air and membrane

NORDIC PAVILLION

Proposed in 1970 by Sverre Fehn for Norway's pavillion at the Osaka World Expos, Norway focused on environmental protection in industrialized societies, seen in Fehn's proposed design. The structure is composed of a hard exterior shell that protects the interior from the traditional exterior hazards, while the inside is composed of two balloons that protect the interior from industrial air, acting as an artifical lung.

BUBBLE WOMAN SHOW

The artist walks around a space, enveloped by a bubble, she then invites a member of the audience to join her within the bubble
“Share the air, the emptiness, the fragility... There are no references, nor indications that allow us to place ourselves. Almost an uncertain space, almost another world. The exterior is vaguely intuited ... the interior too.”

A Living Door of the Museum

Marina Abramavid & Uwe Laysiepen, 1977. A pair of naked actors stand facing each other in a doorway, forcing peopole to move between them to enter a space

Ant Farm

Jesse Seegers, 2016. An inflatable expanding into a existing hard architecture. Occupiable Inflatable

Snowballing Doorway

Alex Schweder, 2008. An flexible soft architecture, shifting from an occupiable archway to the inverse, blocking the pathway

FLOATING MACHINE

Yi's project investigates the ideas of ecological complex ecosystems with artifical intelligence. The exhibition space is coded with scents of different times and spaces. These two independant variables collaboate week to week creating an ever changing expereince for both the user and inflatables. Primary blend of techology and biology.

ORIGAMI INFLATABLE STRUCTURES

An expandable shelter that holds its shape without costant pressure. Proposed as an emergency shelter solution for disaster zones, but also for lunar and mars expeditions. Built from triangular panels, they create closed stable spaces, easy to fold flat for eaasy transportation.
